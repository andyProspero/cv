### Other skills, software
Since 2008, it has been periodically required to draw something in a graphic editor. I am engaged in the assembly of UI /UX design, and the rendering of logos, banners and other things up to design systems and corporate identity entirely. I learned to make up in 2008, at the time of IE5/6, and that's it. Now that we have Chromium core for all popular browsers, the layout has ceased to be so complex and responsible, nevertheless, the skills remain.

When working on new projects, I use a bunch of Figma and PhpStorm. Sometimes layouts in PSD or other formats are sent to me for layout, I don't take it into my head, I just work and make up at least from a PNG image, substituting it on the background of the page and just using the pixel-perfect principle the old-fashioned way, as far as possible.

Also in new projects, when making up, I try to use the Bootstrap framework of the latest stable version, which has almost everything you need to build a beautiful user interface with a content slider, various menus, dynamic areas and hints.

In design, when choosing a style, if it does not contradict the requirements in the task, I choose between the trends of the current year. My last project was drawn in Neumorphism style, and the next one will probably be in Glassmorphism style. For a project aimed at children, I would suggest today perhaps the Lowpoly style as the most elegant and easy to understand, while intricate. However, the best design is the one that is not noticeable with all its convenience.
